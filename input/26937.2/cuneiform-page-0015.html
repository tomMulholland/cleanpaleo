Deane Quarry Locafity (loc. 3, table 1)

General.â€” The Deane Quarry Locality is in an inactive marble quarry which exposes approximately the upper one half to two thirds of the Holston Formation. The importance of this locality lies in the manner in which the upper 20 m (70 ft) of the Holston is exposed. As shown in plate 2, a series of horizontal platforms are separated from one another by irregular steps or low vertical walls. The reef masses are exposed so that three-dimensional relationships can be studied. The cores are also associated with cross-bedded calcarenite, but here the cores are interconnecting, anastomosing masses. Plate 2-A shows the relative proportion of core and flank deposits in the outcrop. Individual cores stood as much as 3 to 6 m (10-20 ft) above adjacent sea-bottom as de- termined by tracing individual cross beds downward away from the cores.

The core was lithifie'1 at the time of deposition by the binding activity of incrusting Bryozoa as evidenced by vertical faces at the edge of cores encnrsted by large bryozoan colonies (see pl. 2-B and l-K). Occasional angular intraclasts of core lithology in adjacent flank de- posits also inclicate early lithification of core. We conclude that the cores represent hard structures which certainly had the potential of being wave-resistant. As in the Alcoa Highway reef core, the incrusting bryo- zoa of the Deane Quarry cores functioned as frame-work builders as well as binders in constructing these reefs.
