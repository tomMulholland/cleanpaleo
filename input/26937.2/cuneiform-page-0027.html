A comparison of the Holston reef with other Ordovician reefs reveals some significant differences. Although the individual reef cores are smaller in the Holston than in some other examples, the Holston cores are not separated bodies as in other Ordovician reefs. Instead the cores studied here are interconnected in three dimensions and, with associated Hank deposits, probably form the largest single reef mass of Ordovician age reported to date. The location of this mass at a shelf edge contrasts with the on-shelf location of other Ordovician reefs. At lower taxonomic levels (generic or specific) the Holston reef is much more diverse than other Ordovician reefs, though at the phylum level fewer groups are involved in the Holston. The apparent absence of stromatoporoids, corals, and algae from the Holston reef biota is puzzling and contrasts markedly with the abundance of these groups in other Ordovician reefs.
