HISTORICAL PERSPECTIVE

The term "Holston Marble" was first applied by Keith in the Knoxville Folio (1895) for the coarse-grained, white to pink to red carbonate above the' Lenoir Formation and below his Tellico Sand- stone. He did not include a discussion nor designate a type section in that or any of the later folios. Ulrich (1911), in his reinterpretation of the Paleozoic, considered the Holston of various strike belts to be contentporaneous and to represent deposition in a single trough. Ulrich failed to accept the possibility of lateral facies changes in the Paleozoic section.

Gordon (1924) accepted Keith's classification and described the distribution and general lithologic character of the Holston. Gordon (p. 29) was the first to suggest a reefal origin for these rocks.
